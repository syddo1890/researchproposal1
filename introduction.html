Introduction

For this research project my aim is to look deeper into cryptocurrency more specifically accounts such as Bitcoin and to observe their want within the community for a more environmentally stable future. I plan to conduct research within “Cyber-Ethnography”, which focuses on participant observation but in chat rooms, online servers, and online interviewing or other virtual data collection. This method will help me do the groundwork of research for those who will later come and create this software for a new future. Within this proposal, I plan on using three different works from the internet to better understand the problem I am addressing. First, Elizabeth Kolbert’s “Why Bitcoin Is Bad for the Environment” to better explain the physical reasons behind why cryptocurrency will have a large negative impact. Next, Susanne Köhler and Massimo Pizzol’s “Life Cycle Assessment of Bitcoin Mining.” mimics a similar study done to weigh the long-term effects of bitcoin mining. And finally, Christine Hine’s novel Virtual Ethnography, where the chapter titled The Virtual Objects of Ethnography will be my main focus. Throughout this proposal, my hope is that I can relay my process for integrating the crypto community into the overarching project of creating a more sustainable future within cryptocurrency and its power usage.
